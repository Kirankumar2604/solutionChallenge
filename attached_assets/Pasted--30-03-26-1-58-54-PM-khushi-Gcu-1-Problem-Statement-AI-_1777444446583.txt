[30/03/26, 1:58:54 PM] khushi (Gcu): 1.⁠ ⁠Problem Statement
AI systems used in hiring, loans, and healthcare often learn from historical data that contains hidden biases, leading to unfair decisions.
These systems can unintentionally discriminate against groups based on gender, income, caste, etc., even if not explicitly programmed to do so.
Most current AI models prioritize accuracy over fairness, ignoring ethical implications of their predictions.
Bias is usually identified after deployment, by which time harm may already be done.
There is a need for a system that can detect and correct bias in real-time during prediction, ensuring fairness without major loss in performance.
[30/03/26, 1:58:54 PM] khushi (Gcu): Existing Methods
Bias is measured using fairness metrics like Demographic Parity, Equal Opportunity, and Disparate Impact, but these are mostly used for analysis rather than action.
Pre-processing methods try to remove bias by modifying the dataset (e.g., balancing data), but they depend heavily on data quality.
In-processing methods introduce fairness constraints during training, but they require changing the model itself and are complex to implement.
Post-processing methods adjust predictions after training, but they are static and not adaptive to real-time inputs.
Overall, existing methods are not real-time, not dynamic, and lack transparency, making them less effective in real-world deployment.
[30/03/26, 1:58:54 PM] khushi (Gcu): 3.⁠ ⁠Proposed Solution
Develop FairLens, a system that monitors AI predictions and detects bias at the moment a decision is made (real-time).
Introduce a Bias Detection Engine that continuously checks fairness metrics for each prediction or batch.
Add a Bias Mitigation Layer that dynamically adjusts predictions (e.g., threshold tuning or re-weighting) if bias is detected.
Provide an Explainability Dashboard to show why a decision was made and whether it was corrected for fairness.
The novelty lies in combining real-time detection + automatic correction + explainability, making the system proactive rather than reactive.
[30/03/26, 1:58:54 PM] khushi (Gcu): 4.⁠ ⁠Implementation & Architecture
Build a pipeline with layers: Input → ML Model → Bias Detection → Bias Mitigation → Output + Dashboard.
Use Scikit-learn for model building and Fairlearn/AIF360 for computing fairness metrics.
Implement real-time mitigation by adjusting decision thresholds or probabilities dynamically based on fairness checks.
Develop a simple UI using Streamlit to input data, visualize predictions, and compare fairness before and after correction.
Use a dataset (real or synthetic) with known bias (e.g., hiring data) to demonstrate how the system detects and fixes unfair outcomes.
[30/03/26, 1:58:55 PM] khushi (Gcu): 5.⁠ ⁠Probable Results
The system will successfully detect biased predictions in real-time and highlight unfair patterns across groups.
After applying mitigation, there will be a significant improvement in fairness metrics (more balanced outcomes).
There may be a small drop in accuracy, but it will be controlled and acceptable for ethical improvement.
The dashboard will clearly show before vs after results, making the system transparent and easy to understand.
Overall, the project will demonstrate that fair and ethical AI is achievable without sacrificing usability, making it practical for real-world applications.
